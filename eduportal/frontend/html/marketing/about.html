The problem we solve

South Sudan's education information gap

Millions of learners cannot find school directories, past exam papers, or scholarship information — not because it doesn't exist, but because no one has put it in one place.

34% Adult literacy rate — one of the lowest in the world
10 States, each with fragmented education data
500+ Schools with no unified public directory
50+ Scholarships that most learners never hear about
"Every learner in South Sudan deserves access to the same information, regardless of whether they live in Juba or in a remote county with a 2G connection." — Solomon Thiong Garang Leek, Founder

Our mission

One platform. Every learner. Every state.

EduPortal South Sudan was built to fix the information gap. Free, verified, and optimised for low-bandwidth connections across all 10 states.

Verified Information

Every school listing, scholarship, and announcement is reviewed by our admin team before it goes live. No misinformation, no outdated data.

Low-Bandwidth First

Designed to work on 2G connections. Lightweight pages, compressed assets, and offline-friendly study material downloads.

Always Free

No subscription, no paywall, no hidden fees. EduPortal is and will always be free for every learner, parent, and teacher in South Sudan.

What drives us

Mission, Vision & Values

The principles behind every decision we make in building EduPortal South Sudan.

Mission

Why we exist

To eliminate the education information gap in South Sudan by providing every learner, parent, teacher, and school with free, verified, and accessible digital tools — regardless of location or internet speed.

Vision

Where we are going

A South Sudan where every learner — from Juba to the most remote county — has equal access to quality education information, and where no scholarship goes unclaimed because a student simply didn't know it existed.

Values

The principles behind every decision

01

Equity of Access

Every feature, every piece of information, and every tool on EduPortal must be equally accessible to a student in Juba and a student in a remote rural county.

02

Radical Transparency

We verify every listing before it goes live. We do not publish unverified schools, scholarships, or announcements — accuracy is non-negotiable.

03

Free, Always

EduPortal will never charge learners, parents, teachers, or schools. The platform exists to serve South Sudan's education sector, not to profit from it.

04

Built for the Context

South Sudan's infrastructure is unique. Every technical decision — from page weight to offline support — is made with 2G connectivity and low-end devices in mind.

05

Community First

EduPortal is built with and for the South Sudanese education community. Feedback from students, teachers, and NGOs shapes every update we ship.

06

Quality Over Speed

We would rather launch fewer features that work reliably than many features that fail learners. Reliability in low-connectivity environments is a quality standard.

How we got here

Our journey

From a single observation to a platform serving every state in South Sudan.

2023 — The idea

Solomon Thiong Garang Leek, a South Sudanese student at African Leadership University, identifies the education information gap as a solvable problem.

2024 — Research & design

Interviews with students, parents, teachers, and NGO officers across 5 states shape the platform's core features and role-based access model.

2025 — Platform build

Full-stack development begins. School directory, study materials repository, scholarship finder, and announcement board are built and tested.

2026 — Launch

EduPortal South Sudan launches publicly — free for every learner across all 10 states, with NGO and school admin portals live from day one.
